About me


I believe that what makes humans unique is our ability to change the world - through our skills, passions and drive, we proactively shape the world into a better one for those who follow. Throughout my years, I have endeavoured to equip myself with the skills necessary to make a change. I am passionate in engineering and programming, having partaken in numerous local and international competitions such as First Lego League (FLL), and RoboRave Australia, deepening my knowledge in the STEM field. I regularly practice empathy and leadership during my years as a Peer Support Leader and Class Chairperson at school. I let my discipline and critical thinking shine through being a constant top scorer in my school, having clinched the Best in history award twice in my secondary years. Most importantly, I am never satisfied with "good enough", for I always strive to become a better version of myself every day. No matter what challenges I face ahead, through the fire and the flames will I emerge triumphant.


Achievements


Singapore VEX V5RC Championships 2025


My team and I were especially eager for this competition, being our last one in the CCA. During the competition, I adapted to and overcame many unforseen challenges, such as the malfunctioning of certain functions of the robot and difficult alliance partners. We fostered strong connections within the VEX community, supporting both allies and competitors. Our positive attitude managed to earn us the "Inspire Award," recognizing our spirit throughout the event!


Singapore VEX Tournament 2024


SingVEX 2024 was my first VEX Robotics competition. With months of building, testing, and refining behind our backs, my team made it to the Round of 16. We demonstrated strong collaboration both within our team and with alliance partners, and were proud to receive the Think Award for our innovative robot design! Through this experience, I sharpened my engineering skills, learned to adapt under pressure, and gained valuable insights into real-world problem-solving.


RoboRave Australia 2024


As my first overseas competition, RoboRave was both exciting and intense. I dedicated countless hours to designing, building, and refining my robot to be able to stand among my competitors from around the world. The competition tested my resilience, problem-solving, and teamwork, and despite the challenges, my team secured 3rd place in the High School Alpine Open Category!


Singapore Science and Engineering Fair 2024


Originally a school project, our investigation into how carbon dioxide affects temperature impressed our teachers enough to qualify us for the SSEF finals. This opportunity taught us how to plan experiments rigorously, communicate findings effectively, and refine our work to a professional standard.


Perse Coding Team Challenge Round 1 2024


I spent numerous hours studying hard for the PCTC, diligently mastering my python essentials, skills, concepts and algorithmic thinking. Despite working under pressure, I collaborated well with my teammate and exercised our critical thinking beautifully to achieve a Distinction award.


National Olympiad in Informatics 2025


Preparing for NOI strengthened my logical thinking and problem-solving mindset. I learned to approach unfamiliar problems methodically, break them into smaller parts, and think several steps ahead. Through countless hours of practice, I not just reinforced by python knowledge, but I also developed sharper intuition and the ability to reason under time pressure — all essential skills for computational thinking.


Singapore Energy Grand Challenge Youth 2023


In the SEGC competition, we were challenged to envision daily life in Singapore in 2050 — with a focus on how energy would be sustainably generated and used. Using Minecraft as our creative platform, we built a detailed model of our future city, blending innovation with environmental responsibility. Through this project, we deepened our understanding of clean energy, climate awareness, and sustainable urban living — and were proud to achieve 3rd place in the competition.



Passion Projects


Parents Guardpost


Developed Parents Guardpost in collaboration with CyberYouth Singapore and SUTD — a website that links children's social media accounts to their parents' to detect and flag potentially predatory behaviour. This was my first experience with HTML and CSS, where I led the programming team while specialising in the front-end department, collaborated with students from other institutions, and learned web development through hands-on problem solving. We were awarded the Trailblazing Solution Award in Stage I of STEAMunity and enhanced the project further in Stage II.


Dyslexhelp


Co-developed Dyslexhelp, a project designed to support individuals with dyslexia by identifying dyslexic handwriting and providing targeted practice exercises to improve it over time. I led the front-end development, but I also gained exposure to working with APIs, machine learning concepts, and real-world datasets which deepened my understanding of how technology can be used to create inclusive, impactful solutions.
